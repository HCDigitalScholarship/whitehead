Becoming an Ally

Lessons from John Whitehead's Life

Next

How did Whitehead develop a sense of Allyship?

John Cunningham Whitehead (1922 – 2015) dedicated his life to bettering the ones of others. He was a successful leader in many ways, and in doing so, he discovered the power of allyship and his role as an agent of privilege. Whether it was in public service, entrepreneurial innovation, philanthropy, or at Haverford College, Whitehead’s hardworking attitude and moral drive helped him develop a strong sense of social responsibility.

He experienced many moments that were characterized by fear and segregation, where college students were either protesting War or dying in it, and water fountains had an opinion about the color of your skin. Whitehead’s initiative eventually made him become the kind of leader he thought was lacking in many social spheres: one who uses his identity to destroy unjust systems and empower the lives of others who can’t do it alone. Whitehead discovered the process of allyship in subtle ways and in traditional environments that were resistant to change.

Entreprenurial Innovation

Whitehead was a man of many careers and as a result, he had a diverse network of contacts that exposed him to projects and experiences that stimulated his feelings for social justice. His first career was in the Navy service during World War II and then he ventured into business and investment banking. When he returned from the War in 1945, he assisted the Navy in an accounting program at the Harvard Business School (HBS).
